# Personalized, Not Creepy: Personalization Rubric

Reference for one-to-one pages: named-account microsites, executive follow-up pages, account-specific ABM pages, renewal/expansion pages, and post-event 1:1 follow-up experiences.

Use this rubric to classify every personalized element before it goes on the page. Each element is Safe, Risky, or Prohibited. Keep Safe, fix or cut Risky, and never include Prohibited.

The page should feel prepared for the account by someone who did professional homework, never like the account is being watched.

## Decision Test

Apply this to every personalized detail, in order:

1. Provenance: Can you name where this detail came from in one sentence? If not, cut it.
2. Comfort: If you told the recipient exactly where it came from, would they nod or recoil? Nod means likely Safe. Recoil means Risky or Prohibited.
3. Relevance: Does it help the offer land, or is it personalization for its own sake? If it does not help, cut it even when it is Safe.

## The Three Axes

Personalization drifts from safe to creepy along these axes. The further right, the more caution required.

| Axis | Safe end | Creepy end |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source | Public / professional / first-party | Private / personal / scraped |
| Basis | Stated by the account | Inferred about the account |
| Granularity | Role- or account-level | Individual behavioral |

## Safe: Use Freely

Reads as professional preparation. No flagging needed.

| Element | Why it is safe |
| --- | --- |
| Company name, logo, industry from the account's public brand | Public and expected |
| A publicly announced initiative the offer ties to | The account announced it themselves |
| The role or function being addressed, such as "for RevOps leaders" | Job-level, not person-level |
| The account's own usage, milestones, and contract dates with your product | First-party data tied to an existing relationship |
| Meeting date and topics actually discussed in that meeting | Stated, shared context |
| Event name, registered session, and the demo actually shown | First-party, consented |
| The rep's real name and photo as the point of contact | Transparent, human |

## Risky: Fix Before Shipping

Do not ship as-is. Generalize to the Safe level, confirm the source with the user, or cut. If it does not clearly help the offer, cut it.

| Element | Problem | Safer move |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Naming individuals beyond your direct contact | Implies you have mapped their org | Default to role or team; only name people who knowingly gave you their info |
| Referencing a person's online activity, such as "saw your LinkedIn post" | Signals surveillance | Drop it unless it came up in conversation |
| Narrating on-page or web behavior, such as "you visited pricing 3 times" | Exposes tracking | Use it to shape content silently; do not describe the tracking |
| Real-time or oddly specific references, such as "opened this at 11pm" | Feels watched | Cut entirely |
| Inferred firmographics from third-party data, such as headcount or tech stack | Provenance may not be defensible | Use only if you would cite the source; otherwise generalize |
| Comparative framing vs. peers or competitors | Implies you monitor others | Keep benchmarks aggregate and anonymized |

## Prohibited: Never Include

Off-limits regardless of source or how the data was obtained.

| Element | Why it is off-limits |
| --- | --- |
| Personal or family details about any individual | Private; outside the professional relationship |
| Protected characteristics, or anything that reveals them | Discriminatory and intrusive |
| Data from scraping, leaks, or providers whose terms you cannot stand behind | Indefensible provenance |
| Badge-scan, location, or movement data used beyond its stated purpose | Breaks the consent it was collected under |
| Individual behavioral data that exposes one employee to another | Turns the page into a surveillance tool |
| Fabricated personal details invented to seem familiar | Dishonest |
| Sensitive inferences, such as churn risk, financial distress, or hiring freezes, shown back to the account | Presumptuous and damaging |

## Page-Type Quick Reference

Default personalization for each page type this skill covers.

| Page type | Safe by default | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Named-account microsite | Company brand, industry, publicly stated initiatives, role-level framing | Naming individuals beyond your contact; inferred private facts |
| Executive follow-up | Meeting date, topics discussed, materials mapped to the conversation | Anything about the exec found outside the meeting |
| Account-specific ABM | Public initiatives, use-case framing, account team | Individual behavioral tracking surfaced explicitly |
| Renewal / expansion | First-party usage, milestones, contract dates, named team | Seat-level data that exposes individual employees |
| Post-event 1:1 follow-up | Event name, registered session, demo shown | Movement/badge data beyond its stated purpose |

## Pre-Publish Checklist

Before saving to a live board:

1. List every personalized element on the page with its data source.
2. Confirm each Risky element with the user, or remove it.
3. Remove any element whose source you cannot name.
4. Present the full list of personalized elements and their sources for explicit confirmation.

A page must never publish with personalization whose provenance is unverified.
